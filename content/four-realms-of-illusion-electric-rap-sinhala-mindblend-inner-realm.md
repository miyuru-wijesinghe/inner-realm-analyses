Line 5:
Sinhala: "පෙනෙනා දෙය තුල ඇසෙනා දෙය තුල දැනෙනා දෙය තුල සිත සිරවී නම්"
English Translation: "If the mind is trapped in what is seen, heard, and felt"
Explanation for English Audience:
Now we go deeper into HOW we get trapped. We mistake sensory input-based mind creations for reality. Here's the crucial point: it's not just that we misinterpret sensory data - it's that our MIND CREATES an entire reality based on sensory input, and we mistake that mental creation for what's actually "out there."
You see red light wavelengths → your mind CREATES the experience "red" → you think "red exists out there as an objective thing." But "red" is your mind's creation based on sensory input, not reality itself.
You hear sound vibrations → your mind CREATES the experience "beautiful music" → you think "that music IS beautiful." But "beautiful" is your mind's creation based on auditory input, not an inherent property of the sound waves.
The trap isn't just misunderstanding sensory input - it's mistaking the mind's creative interpretation of sensory input for objective reality. We're prisoners not of the senses, but of the mind's constructed world that it builds FROM sensory input.

When you listen to your own song, three things happen:

Sensory input: Sound waves hit your ear (ඉන්ද්‍රිය ආදානය)
Mind's creation: Your mind constructs "music," "melody," "meaning" (මනසේ නිර්මාණය)
The trap: You think this constructed experience IS reality (උගුල)

In Buddhist terms: Some forms (රූප) arise with sensory contact, and those become the starting point for the mind's construction of experience. Not all රූප involves sensory objects - there are pure material forms. But when forms DO arise through sensory contact (eye meeting visible form, ear meeting sound, etc.), those sensory-based forms become the foundation upon which the mind (නාම) builds its elaborate constructions of "reality."
බෞද්ධ වචනවලින්: ඇතැම් රූප ඉන්ද්‍රිය ස්පර්ශය සමඟ ඇති වෙනවා, සහ ඒවා මනසේ අත්දැකීම් නිර්මාණය සඳහා ආරම්භක ස්ථානය බවට පත්වෙනවා. සියලු රූප ඉන්ද්‍රිය වස්තූන් ඇතුළත් කරන්නේ නැහැ - පිරිසිදු ද්‍රව්‍යමය රූප තිබේ. නමුත් රූප ඉන්ද්‍රිය ස්පර්ශය හරහා ඇතිවන විට (ඇස දෘශ්‍ය රූපය හමුවීම, කණ ශබ්දය හමුවීම, ආදිය), ඒ ඉන්ද්‍රිය-පදනම් රූප මනස (නාම) "යථාර්ථය" පිළිබඳ විස්තීර්ණ නිර්මාණ ගොඩනගන පදනම බවට පත්වෙනවා.
Are you hearing vibrations, or are you trapped in your mind's elaborate construction of "good/bad," "successful/failed," "beautiful/ugly" built upon those vibrations? The sensory forms (ඉන්ද්‍රිය රූප) are just the starting point - the real trap is in what the mind constructs FROM that starting point.
The Yaman solo: organized air pressure changes (රූප arising through ear contact) become the starting point for your mind's construction of "raga," "emotion," "beauty," "my composition," "success/failure" (නාම constructions). The trap is thinking those mental constructions exist IN the sound rather than recognizing them as mind-made elaborations BASED ON the sound.

Line 6:
Sinhala: "හිතනා එකකට ඇසෙනා එකකට පෙනීම කිසිදා නොම හමුවී නම්"
English Translation: "If what is thought, what is heard, and what is seen never meet"
Explanation for English Audience:
This is PROFOUND. You think one thing, hear another, see yet another - but they're disconnected. You HEAR your teacher say "let go," you SEE the words, you THINK "yes, I understand," but they never actually converge into direct experience. It's like reading a recipe (seeing), hearing someone describe the taste (hearing), thinking about it (thinking) - but never actually tasting the food. Fragmented existence.
Sinhala Audience සඳහා විස්තරය:
මෙය ගැඹුරුයි. ඔයා එක දෙයක් හිතනවා, තවත් දෙයක් අහනවා, තවත් දෙයක් දකිනවා - නමුත් ඒවා විසන්ධි වෙලා. ඔයා ඔයාගේ ගුරුවරයා "අතහැර දමන්න" කියන එක අහනවා, වචන දකිනවා, "ඔව්, මට තේරෙනවා" කියල හිතනවා, නමුත් ඒවා කවදාවත් සෘජු අත්දැකීමක් බවට එක්වෙන්නේ නැහැ. ආහාර වට්ටෝරුවක් කියවනවා (දැකීම), කවුරුහරි රසය විස්තර කරනවා අහනවා (ඇසීම), ඒ ගැන හිතනවා (සිතීම) වගේ - නමුත් කවදාවත් ඇත්තටම ආහාරය රස බලන්නේ නැහැ. ඛණ්ඩනය වූ පැවැත්ම.
Song-Connected Example / ගීතයට සම්බන්ධ උදාහරණය:
Think about creating THIS song: you THOUGHT about Buddhist concepts, you HEARD melodies in your mind, you SAW the lyrics forming - but when did they truly MEET and become ONE creative flow? That moment of unity is rare and precious. Most of the time we live fragmented. Your song is asking: when do all senses and mind actually align? That alignment IS the meditative state. The Yaman solo represents that unified flow - no gap between intention, execution, and listening.

Line 7:
Sinhala: "දැනෙනා එක වෙන පෙනෙනා එක වෙන හිතීම පමණක් මම වී අවසන්"
English Translation: "What is felt is separate, what is seen is separate, only thinking becomes 'me' and ends"
Explanation for English Audience:
When senses don't integrate, we retreat into pure thought. "I think, therefore I am" becomes our prison. You reduce yourself to a thinking machine. You're not living life - you're thinking ABOUT life. Feeling disconnected from seeing, seeing disconnected from hearing - only the narrator in your head seems real. That narrator IS the ego, and it's all you have left.
Sinhala Audience සඳහා විස්තරය:
ඉන්ද්‍රියයන් ඒකාබද්ධ නොවන විට, අපි පිරිසිදු චින්තනයට පසුබසිනවා. "මම සිතමි, එබැවින් මම සිටිමි" අපේ බන්ධනාගාරය බවට පත්වෙනවා. ඔයා ඔයාව සිතන යන්ත්‍රයකට අඩු කරනවා. ඔයා ජීවත් වෙන්නේ නැහැ - ජීවිතය ගැන සිතනවා. දැනීම දැකීමෙන් විසන්ධි, දැකීම ඇසීමෙන් විසන්ධි - ඔයාගේ හිසේ කතාකරු පමණක් සැබෑ වගේ පෙනෙනවා. ඒ කතාකරු තමයි අහංකාරය, සහ ඔයාට ඉතිරි වෙලා තියෙන්නේ ඒක පමණයි.
