Sinhala: 
"දැන ගත් බව නොව එහි හට ගත් හැටි ඉස්මතු වේ නම්"
English Translation: 
"Not the knowing itself, but if the way it arose becomes clear in this moment"
Explanation for English Audience:
Instead of just knowing "I am aware," watch HOW that awareness is being born right now. Like watching a flower bloom frame-by-frame rather than just seeing "a flower." This line sets up the entire song's theme: the danger of getting stuck in awareness itself.

Line 2:
Sinhala: "දැන ගත් බව තුල දුක සැප මැද ගොළුවී සිරවී නම්"
English Translation: "If trapped and entangled in pleasure and pain within that knowing"
Explanation for English Audience:
This is the first trap. You become aware, but then use that awareness to chase "good" meditative states and avoid "bad" ones. You're spiritually awake but emotionally still playing the same game: seeking pleasure, avoiding pain. Your awareness becomes just another tool for desire.

Line 3:
Sinhala: "දැන ගත් බව තුල නැවතත් මගකට මුල් සැරසී නම්"
English Translation: "If within that knowing, the beginning of a new path is prepared again"
Explanation for English Audience:
Hope! Even trapped awareness can become a stepping stone. When you realize "I'm using awareness as an ego tool," that realization itself is the seed of a new journey. It's recursive - awareness watching awareness, then awareness of THAT watching. Each layer brings you closer to understanding there's no "watcher" at all.

Line 4:
Sinhala: "දැන ගත් බව තුල දැන ගත් බව යළි මම වී අවසන්"
English Translation: "Within that knowing, that knowing itself becomes 'me' again and ends"
Explanation for English Audience:
The ultimate trap! You transcend thinking, only to get stuck in "the one who transcended." You stop identifying with thoughts, but now identify with awareness. It's like escaping a small cage only to find yourself in a bigger one. The ego is clever - it can even make "no-ego" into an ego. This is where most meditators plateau.
